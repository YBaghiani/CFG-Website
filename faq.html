What's it all about?

Refugees are too often seen as news stories or statistics. However, they are real people just like you and me. Cuppa Connection brings people together and facilitate conversations with refugees to ultimately foster greater understanding and empathy in London.

Which cafes in London do you meet up?

We meet up at independent cafes all across London. Locations and times are varied so that everyone in London has a chance to attend.

How many people come to a tea time?

The host, a refugee and up to four others join for each tea time.

How can I share pictures and stories from a tea time?

As long as you have permission from people in photographs please share these on social media, you can use the campaign hashtag #CuppaConnection or tag Cuppa Conection. We’re on Facebook, Instagram and Twitter.

Where can I find reliable facts and figures about refugees?

British Red Cross has some useful facts and figures or a blog post about three key facts.

Is Cuppa Connection not labelling people?

Refugees are like anyone - complex multi-faceted people with various experiences and stories. We agreed during consultation that using the term “refugee” worked as a simple initial way of communicating the campaign and the campaign itself should allow people to get to know one another as people, rather than labels.

How do you find refugees to come?

We work closely with refugee community organisations.
